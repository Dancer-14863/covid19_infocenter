Pfizer-BioNTech Covid-19 Vaccine




Information about Pfizer-BioNTech Covid-19 Vaccine

Pfizer-BioNTech Covid-19 vaccine is a type of mRNA vaccine that is manufactured by Pfizer Inc and BioNTech. The Pfizer-BioNTech Covid-19 vaccine is an unapproved vaccine that may prevent Covid-19. The FDA has authorized the emergency use of the vaccine to prevent Covid-19 in individuals 16 years of age and older under an Emergency Use Authorization (EUA). The vaccine uses messenger RNA, a genetic material that our cells read to make proteins.




What is mRNA?

MRNA vaccines are a new type of vaccine to protect against diseases. Unlike other vaccines that uses weakened or inactivated germ, mRNA instruct our cells to create a protein that triggers an immune response in our bodies. The protein is called “spike protein” which is found on the surface of the virus that causes Covid-19. Once the immune response is triggered, our body will produce antibodies that will protect us from getting infected by the actual virus when it enters our body.

Additional infomation

  • The vaccine has been shown to prevent Covid-19 after getting 2 doses given 21 days apart. The duration of protection from the virus is still unknown.
  • Evidences from clinical trials shows that the vaccine was 95% effective at preventing laboratory-confirmed Covid-19 illness in people without evidence of previous infection.
  • Participants may experience side effects after getting the vaccine injection. The most common side effects are pain at the injection site, tiredness, headache, muscle pain, chills, joint pain and fever. These side effects usually lasted for a few days.
  • The vaccine might cause severe allergic reaction within a few minutes to an hour after getting a dose of the vaccine. Severe allergic reaction can include the following: difficulty breathing, swelling of your face and throat, fast heartbeat, bad rash all over your body, dizziness and weakness.

Who should get the vaccine and who shouldn't

  • The vaccine is recommended for people 16 years of age and older.
  • People who had a severe or immediate allergic reaction to any of the ingredient in the vaccine should not get the vaccine.
  • People who had a severe or immediate allergic reaction after getting the first dose of vaccine should not get the second dose.
